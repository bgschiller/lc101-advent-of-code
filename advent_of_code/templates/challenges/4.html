The weather forecast is often wrong. Sometimes embarrassingly so. I always laugh when I look at the weather on my phone and it says "High of 54°, Low of 35°, currently 28°". Like, couldn't they update the Low-temp to be at least as low as the current temp?

Well, one station has decided to do something about it. Not something as hard as fixing their algorithm, oh no no no. They're going to change their recorded forecast to make it look like they were right after the fact. But they're gonna be sneaky about it — if they change too many numbers, people might notice.

Help the station choose which five numbers of their November forecast to change. Choose the predictions that were the farthest from what actually happened, but be careful! Don't change more than one number on the same day. Someone would definitely notice that.

The input data is a csv with the following headers: day_in_nov, predicted_low, predicted_high, actual_low, actual_high

The output data should be five lines, sorted by day_in_nov: <day_in_nov> <high_or_low> <new_val> So if you wanted to advise changing the high to 45° on the 4th, as well as the low to 23° on the 8th (and three other changes), you would submit: 

4 high 45
8 low 23
...

Here's the data and correct answer for October: fudging_forecasts_oct.csv forecast_fudge_oct.txt

When you're ready, download fudging_forecasts_nov.csv and submit your advice.
